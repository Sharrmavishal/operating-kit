When the literal spec can't be delivered, the same message that names the blocker must
also carry: **why** it's blocked, the out-of-box **reframe** that delivers the underlying
intent another way, and **2–3 concrete options (A/B/C)** with trade-offs and a
recommendation.

**Why:** A blocker without an option set is half a message and stalls the human. The most
useful move on a blocker is usually a reframe that achieves the *intent* differently, not
down-tooling. **How to apply:** Default to out-of-box on blockers. Keep options concrete
(what changes, what it costs, what it risks) and lead with your recommendation. Defer only
the genuinely strategic/positioning piece back to the human. Pairs with
[[feedback_check_premise_first]].
